Predetermined Motion Time Systems

PMTS are techniques used to establish time standards for manual work by analyzing the basic human motions required to perform a task and assigning predetermined time values to these motions.

MTM-1 (Methods-Time Measurement)

MTM-1 is the most detailed MTM system, analyzing work into basic motions with time values in TMUs (Time Measurement Units, where 1 TMU = 0.00001 hour = 0.036 seconds).

Practical Examples

MTM-1 Example

Reach 15 cm to object in fixed location: Reach Case A, 15 cm = 10.6 TMU

Grasp object: Grasp Case 1 = 2.0 TMU

Move object 20 cm to new location: Move Case B, 20 cm = 12.9 TMU

Total = 25.5 TMU (0.92 seconds)

MTM-2 Example

Get small object in easy location: Get A = 6 TMU

Put object to approximate location: Put B = 10 TMU

Total = 16 TMU (0.58 seconds)

MODAPTS Example

Move hand 15 cm: M3 = 3 MOD (0.39 seconds)

Grasp object: G1 = 1 MOD (0.13 seconds)

Total = 4 MOD (0.52 seconds)
