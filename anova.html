Perform One-Way Analysis of Variance to compare means across multiple groups

Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)

ANOVA is a statistical method used to test differences between two or more means. It compares the variance between groups to the variance within groups to determine if any significant differences exist.

F = (Between-group variability) / (Within-group variability)

Where F is the test statistic that follows an F-distribution. A large F-value indicates that the differences between group means are greater than would be expected by chance.
