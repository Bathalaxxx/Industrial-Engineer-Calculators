Gage Repeatability and Reproducibility

Gage R&R is a statistical tool that measures the amount of variation in the measurement system arising from the measurement device and the people taking the measurements.

% Gage R&R = (σ_gage / σ_total) × 100%

Where:

  • Repeatability - Equipment variation (same operator, same part)
  • Reproducibility - Appraiser variation (different operators, same part)
  • Gage R&R - Combined repeatability and reproducibility
  • Part Variation - Variation between different parts

Practical Examples

Example 1: Acceptable Measurement System

% Gage R&R: 12% | NDC: 11

Interpretation: The measurement system is acceptable for process control and analysis.

Example 2: Marginal Measurement System

% Gage R&R: 20% | NDC: 7

Interpretation: The measurement system may be acceptable for some applications but should be improved.

Example 3: Unacceptable Measurement System

% Gage R&R: 35% | NDC: 3

Interpretation: The measurement system needs improvement before it can be used for decision making.
