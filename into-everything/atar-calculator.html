How ATAR is Calculated

The Australian Tertiary Admission Rank (ATAR) is a number between 0.00 and 99.95 that indicates a student's position relative to all other students in their age group. Here's how it's calculated:

ATAR = f(Sum of best 4 scaled subject scores + 10% of next best subjects)

Key Components of ATAR Calculation

Subject Scaling +

Subjects are scaled to account for differences in difficulty and competition. Scaling ensures that students aren't advantaged or disadvantaged based on their subject choices.

Scaling factors are determined by statistical analysis of student performance across different subjects and are adjusted annually.

Generally, mathematics, sciences, and languages scale higher than humanities and arts subjects.

English Requirements +

All Australian states require satisfactory completion of an English subject for ATAR eligibility.

In most states, English must be included in your ATAR calculation, even if it's not one of your highest-scoring subjects.

The specific English requirements vary by state, so check with your state's tertiary admissions center.

Bonus Points +

Many universities offer bonus points for certain subjects, geographical locations, or equity reasons.

Bonus points are typically added to your ATAR for admission purposes but don't change your actual ATAR.

Common bonus point schemes include:

  • Subject-specific bonuses (e.g., for mathematics or science subjects)
  • Regional bonuses for students from rural areas
  • Equity bonuses for disadvantaged students
  • Elite athlete or performer programs
State Variations +

While the ATAR is nationally recognized, each state has slight variations in how it's calculated:

  • NSW (HSC): Based on the average of the best 10 units, including at least 2 units of English
  • Victoria (VCE): Calculated from the primary four studies (each worth 50 points) plus 10% of the next best two studies
  • Queensland (QCE): Based on the best five general subjects completed for the QCE
  • Western Australia (WACE): Calculated from the scaled scores of the best four subjects, which must include English
  • South Australia (SACE): Based on the best 80 credits of study at Stage 2 level, with specific subject requirements

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good ATAR score? +

A "good" ATAR depends on your university and course preferences:

  • 95.00+: Highly competitive courses (Medicine, Law, Dentistry)
  • 85.00-94.95: Competitive courses (Engineering, Commerce, Psychology)
  • 70.00-84.95: Many popular courses (Education, Nursing, Arts)
  • 50.00-69.95: Entry to many universities with a range of options
  • Below 50.00: Pathway programs and alternative entry options available
Which subjects scale the highest? +

Generally, subjects that are more difficult and have more academically competitive cohorts scale higher. These typically include:

  • Mathematics Extension 2
  • Mathematics Extension 1
  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Languages (especially less commonly taught languages)
  • Economics

However, it's important to choose subjects you're good at and interested in, as performing well in a lower-scaling subject often yields better results than performing poorly in a high-scaling subject.

How does English affect my ATAR? +

English plays a crucial role in ATAR calculation:

  • All states require satisfactory completion of an English subject for ATAR eligibility
  • In most states, English must be included in your ATAR calculation, even if it's not one of your top subjects
  • Different English levels (Standard, Advanced, Extension) have different scaling factors
  • Performing well in English can significantly boost your ATAR, as it's a mandatory component
What ATAR do I need for medicine? +

Medicine is one of the most competitive fields in Australia. Typical ATAR requirements:

  • Undergraduate Medicine: 99.00+ ATAR typically required, plus UCAT exam and interview
  • Graduate Medicine: Requires completion of any bachelor's degree, plus GAMSAT exam

Specific requirements vary by university, and most use a combination of ATAR, entrance exams, interviews, and sometimes portfolio assessments.

Can I improve my ATAR after Year 12? +

Yes, there are several pathways to improve your ATAR or gain university entry:

  • University Pathway Programs: Foundation studies or diploma programs that lead to degree admission
  • TAFE/VET Courses: Completing a Certificate IV or higher can provide entry to university
  • Bridging Courses: Short courses to meet specific subject prerequisites
  • Mature Age Entry: For students over 21, different entry criteria often apply
  • Retaking Subjects: Some states allow you to retake subjects to improve scores

Methodology

Our calculator uses the following approach to estimate your ATAR:

  1. Subjects are scaled based on historical scaling data from each state
  2. The best 4-5 subjects are selected based on your state's calculation method
  3. A percentage of additional subjects may be included depending on state rules
  4. English requirements are factored in where applicable
  5. The aggregate score is converted to an ATAR using statistical distributions
  6. Bonus points are added if specified

We regularly update our scaling factors and calculation methods based on the latest available data from state admission centers.
