OSHA Noise Dose Calculator

Calculate noise exposure levels and determine compliance with OSHA standards

Noise Dose Calculation

This calculator helps determine OSHA noise dose and Time-Weighted Average (TWA) for workplace noise exposure assessment.

D = 100 × Σ(Cₙ / Tₙ)

Where:

  • D = Noise dose percentage
  • Cₙ = Time spent at a specific noise level
  • Tₙ = Permissible time at that level (T = 8 / 2(L-90)/5)
TWA = 16.61 × log10(D/100) + 90

Where TWA is the 8-hour Time-Weighted Average sound level.

OSHA Requirements: A hearing conservation program is required for all employees exposed to TWA of 85 dB or higher.

Practical Examples

Example 1: Combined Exposures

A worker is exposed to 95 dBA for 3 hours and to 90 dBA for 5 hours.

D = 100 × (3/4 + 5/8) = 100 × (0.75 + 0.625) = 137.5%

This exceeds the OSHA limit of 100% dose.

Example 2: TWA Calculation

For a noise dose of 139.3%:

TWA = 16.61 × log10(139.3/100) + 90 = 92.39 dBA

This exceeds the 85 dBA action level requiring a hearing conservation program.

Example 3: Single Exposure

A worker is exposed to 100 dBA for 1 hour:

Permissible time at 100 dBA: T = 8 / 2(100-90)/5 = 8 / 22 = 2 hours

D = 100 × (1/2) = 50% (within limits)

OSHA Noise Exposure Limits

Noise Level (dBA) Permissible Exposure Time Dose per Hour (%)
90 8 hours 12.5%
92 6 hours 16.7%
95 4 hours 25%
97 3 hours 33.3%
100 2 hours 50%
105 1 hour 100%
110 30 minutes 200%
115 15 minutes 400%
Note: Continuous exposure to noise levels above 115 dBA is not permitted under any circumstances.
