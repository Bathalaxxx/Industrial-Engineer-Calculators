Time Study Analysis

Time study is a work measurement technique for recording the times of performing a task's elements, with the objective of determining the time required to perform the task at a defined standard of performance.

Standard Time = (Normal Time) × (1 + Allowance Factor)
Normal Time = (Observed Time) × (Performance Rating)

Where:

  • Observed Time = Average of recorded times for the task
  • Performance Rating = Assessment of worker's pace (typically 80-120%)
  • Normal Time = Time required at standard performance
  • Allowance Factor = Additional time for rest, delays, etc. (as percentage)
